## Q15. Canonical ordering of subgroups H → the label tiebreaker *(blocks T29)*

**Measured problem**: running `GenerateDataForGerbiestSurjectiveH(O, mu, 1)` for D=6, deg μ=1 twice in a row assigns the labels `6.1.1.2.0.a.1` / `6.1.1.2.0.b.1` to the *opposite* two curves each time. `updateLabels` (`enumerate-H.m:288`) sorts by `PermutationCharacter` and the two curves tie, so the class letter is decided by whatever order `Subgroups(G,KG)` happened to return. The committed data disagrees with both runs. Since LMFDB labels are permanent identifiers, we need a canonical total order.

The two tied curves are distinguishable: `autmuO_norms = {6,1,1,1,1,1}` with generators `{{2,-2,-3,1,0,0,0,0},…}` vs `{3,1,1,1,1,1}` with `{{-1,0,1,1,0,0,0,0},…}`.

1. What should break the tie, mathematically? Modular curves carry an explicit `tiebreaker` column (and the shimura frontend already lists `tiebreaker` among copied-but-unused modcurve columns) — do we want the analogous notion, and what is it here? Candidates: the Atkin–Lehner data (sorted `autmuO_norms` — natural, since these are the refined AL involutions and would give the "w₆ curve" a stable letter relative to the "w₃ curve"); the generator coordinates under a fixed lex order (canonical but basis-dependent and ugly); the fixed-field/CM data of the corresponding quotient.
2. Should the ordering be intrinsic (invariant-based, so independent of the choice of μ and of Magma's enumeration) or is a fixed-choice-of-μ + fixed-algorithm reproducibility enough? (Intrinsic is much more robust — it survives T08's change of μ representative — but needs a complete invariant to sort on.)
3. Practical consequence: the shipped labels in `gps_shimura_test` were produced by the current unstable procedure, so they may not be reproducible at all. Is it acceptable to **relabel the whole table** on the next reload (breaking any existing external references), or must we pin the existing labels and only stabilize going forward?
